This chart shows the representational suspension rate for Oakland Unified School District in 2014.

About 90% are African-American, Hispanic or Latino.

No Suspend empowers Oakland community members by providing data to address escalating infraction and suspension rates among students of color in the Oakland Unified School District. The app will bring awareness using statistics from the California Department of Education to demonstrate the correlation between suspension rates and communities of color. No Suspend app will work in conjunction with existing Oakland Unified School District programs to eliminate racially disproportionate discipline practices.

Share these facts on Twitter:

1/5

Researchers found that 1 out of 5 African American boys in Oakland were suspended from school at least once last year.1

6x

Black males made up 42 percent of suspensions, a suspension rate six times higher than that of white males.1

28%

Percentage of suspensions for OUSD in 2013-2014 are categorized as "other" and therefore ambiguous to the public.2

Make Sure Schools Reflect the Community

Find an OUSD school where you can volunteer and have an impact. Share your career path. Work in an after-school program. Find a young person to mentor.

Support and Encourage Literacy

Whether you are a parent, grandparent, family member, close friend or concerned citizen, take initiative to make sure that the young people in your community are reading.

Lend Your Voice to Elected Leaders

Contact your district school board member and demand more health, social, and academic supports --especially for young people.
